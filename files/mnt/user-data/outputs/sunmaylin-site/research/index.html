Measuring Dollar Funding Shortage
Revise & Resubmit — AEJ: Macroeconomics
Latest Draft
Selected Presentations: IMF Research Department · 12th PhD Conference on International Macroeconomics (Université Paris Nanterre) · HKIMR-AMRO-ECB-ESM-BOFIT Joint Workshop · Midwest Macroeconomics (Lubbock, TX) · FDIC (Washington DC)

I construct a novel measure of dollar funding shortage for a sample of 116 economies during the period 1980–2021. I name this measure the "Dollar Funding Shortage" (DFS) index. The DFS index is a country-level assessment of countries' dollar funding conditions, constructed through text classifications using an NLP model. I document that severe dollar funding shortages are more prevalent than financial crises, and typically happen simultaneously with or precede currency and banking crises. Empirically, I find that such country-level dollar funding shortage adversely affects GDP, bank dollar lending, imports, and exports of firms that are more exposed to dollar financing.

Trade Interventions, Exchange Rate, and Global Value Chains
In Progress
with Rudolfs Bems and Nikhil Patel
Latest Draft Slides
Presentations: IMF · Eastern · Midwest Macro

How does the exchange rate react to trade interventions? We revisit the classical model of trade protectionism's impact on the exchange rate based on a high-frequency global trade actions dataset. By constructing an accurate measure of trade policy shocks at the product-line level, our approach allows quantifying the exchange rate offset on trade interventions. Using the panel local projection method, we show that: 1) a 1 percent increase of uniform import tariff will be offset by a 0.05–0.1 percent appreciation of the home currency; 2) non-trade barriers have a larger impact on exchange rate movements; 3) exchange rate responds differently to tariff announcements versus inception, with the home currency depreciating in between.

Global Value Chains and Export Elasticity: The Role of Dollar Exchange Rate
Draft Coming Soon

Using the World Input-Output (WIOD) and ADB Multi-Regional Input-Output (MRIO) data, this paper re-examines the impact of integration in global value chains (GVC) on the exchange rate elasticity of export volumes. I focus on analyzing linkages under the "Dominant Currency Paradigm," looking at three tracks: the trade-weighted exchange rate (competitiveness channel), the bilateral dollar exchange rate with the export destination country (invoicing channel), and the broad dollar index (financial channel). I show that the dollar invoicing channel dominates the competitiveness channel for explaining the declining exchange rate elasticity of exports, and that GVC integration can make exports more vulnerable to USD funding pressure through the financial channel.

Cross Currency Basis and Bank Lending: A Global and Multi-Country Perspective
In Progress

I propose a common factor approach in a multi-country setting to analyze the triangular relation between cross-currency basis (CCB), cross-border bank flows, and domestic investment. I decompose currency's CCB into 1) a global component and 2) a country-specific component, then compare contributions of each factor and their implications for domestic financial conditions across net creditor and net debtor countries.

The Fed Swap Line and Dollar Shortage of Global Financial Institutions
Dormant (2020)
Draft

This essay evaluates the effectiveness of the Fed swap line facilities in alleviating dollar liquidity stress during the Global Financial Crisis and the Covid-19 pandemic. Using high-frequency weekly auction operation results, I show substantial discrepancies across countries in terms of financial market response to the swap arrangement, and identify key characteristics of countries that benefit most. Strikingly, banks with higher currency mismatch are exposed negatively to the Fed swap facility — suggesting an "informational channel" for announcing a large volume of operation.
